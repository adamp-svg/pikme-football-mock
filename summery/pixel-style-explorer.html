Same football, 8 ways to render it

Each row is a different graphics treatment — not a colour choice but how the world is actually built and lit: flat 2D pixels (what we ship now), Minecraft-style textured blocks, low-poly 3D, cel-shaded toon, clay, paper cut-outs. Columns are the same three things each time — the lobby, the field, and the hero — so you can hold "what we have" against "blocks with texture" and the rest, side by side. Every tile is drawn live in the browser.

How to read this: tiles are live procedural renders that stand in for each graphics pipeline — enough to judge the feel (dimensionality, surface, lighting, silhouette) before committing an artist or an engine. The point of the exercise is the jump from our flat 2D sprites to a textured-block / 3D world. Real assets would be modelled or hand-drawn in the chosen treatment.
